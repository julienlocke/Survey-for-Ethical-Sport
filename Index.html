2016 Survey for Ethical Sport - Results

Thank you for taking time to complete this survey. We will be forwarding the results to as many “agencies” as we can. We believe that people in clubs, in teams, and citizens need to have a chance to express their opinions. If we want our sports to survive, it is important to be heard.

Here is an overview of the results we have collected.

Question 3: With respect to the above, should chronic abusers of the rules of sport, the WADA code, and the IOC charter be sanctioned? Be excused?

Question 3: Comments

  • Sanctions should be much harsher

  • Doping needs a hardline response, otherwise cheaters will continue to cheat even if it costs them a short term ban

  • Sanctions in some cases should be longer, and/or "life" sentences, in cases where use of certain drugs have been shown to have performance enhancement far longer than the sanctions.

  • high level cheats (expo, hgh, blood doping, testosterone, etc) should be banned for life. It embarrassing how some of these federations are getting away with covering up or even supporting doping programs. Sanctions need to be consistent among countries as well.

  • No tolerance policy is the only way to show athletes that our governing bodies are serious about cleaning up the sport. When people keep getting second chances, it undermines the rules and makes people think they can get away with it and still have a future in sport.

  • Chronic abusers of the rules should be banned from competition.

  • otherwise, what is the point of the rules in the first place?

  • If the abuser in question has clearly intended to seek an unfair advantage over his/her competitors, then they should absolutely be sanctioned. If it can be proved that an honest mistake was made by the abuser, there should still be a penalty, but maybe not as harsh as for those who intended to cheat. Overall the penalties need to be clear, severe, and strictly upheld in order to be effective.

  • Yes, for chronic abusers that have significant evidence against them.

  • Excluded from competition and at the same time helped to improve their compliance with WADA and IOC!

  • ABSOLUTELY. i guess i would say they should be sanctioned by the 'rules' however, i do believe there should be discretion among the enforcers too; meaning, if/when someone gets a doping sanction (6 mo or 2 yr) then again is found to be say tampering with a test or bribing a DCO? there should be a rule of sorts "2 strikes" as this is unacceptable behavior which implies consistent rule breaking, consistent lack of integrity for one's sport and lack of respect for all involved. i would like to see far more stringent bans with doping offenses but moreso MULTIPLE offenses.

  • Chronic abuse of rules in sport sacrifices the integrity of the sport. Some people get away with too much and the sports and athletes suffer from their actions and lack of repercussions.

  • If you are caught doping, participating in doping, are helping others dope, you should face the harshest consequences that can be received.   • Chronic abusers should not be allowed to compete. At all. Never again. Cheating is not a forgivable offense.

  • When cheaters are caught more than once they have no regard for the rules, a second doping offense means they should be banned for life. I also believe first time penalties should be more strict.

  • Once you have shown you skirt rules or systematically avoid testing, you should be gone. It's not your right to compete, there are rules that have to be followed.

  • We are, for the most part, adults. One offense Ina critical time can be disastrous in many ways.

  • I believe that abusers should face a penalty.

  • I believe that one positive test should erase their name and any accomplishment from existence even if before the date of the test.

  • Again, the "should" is bothersome. What is the objective? What would be the effect of a two tier sporting system, one with no doping restrictions and one with doping restrictions? .

  • If you are going to have only a drug-free division, it should be a lifetime suspension on first offense. Also, every athlete should have to provide a bond guaranteeing their clean urine-blood on penalty of default.
